Low-Resource Mechanical Ventilator

With the rise of COVID-19 cases, hospitals have been under immense pressure to provide ample resources to be care for hospitalized patients. The most dangerous symptoms experienced by COVID-19 patients are the respiratory ones, in which the virus inflames the lungs, making breathing and oxygen delivery difficult. In addition to the world-wide shortages in PPE, hospitals are experiencing an undersupply of mechanical ventilators, the machines that keep seriously ill patients alive.


Our Problem Statement: We will design a way to provide a mechanical ventilator system with clinician-controlled parameters during a mechanical ventilator shortage in the growing population of emergency patients in US middle-tier hospitals struggling with respiratory infections that is composed of materials readily available in industrialized nation, scalable, compliant with FDA regulations, and integrates with current ventilation procedures.

Considerations

User Needs

  • Control of volume, I/E ratio, respiratory rate (RR), pressure, humidity
  • Detection of volume, I/E ratio, respiratory rate (RR), pressure, humidity
  • Pressure assist detection
  • Causes no additional injury
  • Small resolution for adjustable parameters
  • Filtration of exhalation
  • Ability to manually override
  • Volume control and pressure control capabilities

Payer Needs

  • Rapidly Fabricable
  • Made of common resources
  • Consumes less power than current ventilators
  • Less expensive than current ventilators

Regulatory Needs

  • Minimum lifespan and durability
  • Alarms under failure conditions
  • Compliant with FDA regulations for COVID-19 ventilators
  • Compliant with common hospital practices for ventilators
  • Adheres to minimum and maximum parameters required
