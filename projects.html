BME Design Fellows

Collaborators: Mia de Leon, Nami Reddy, Nick Schiciano

Advisors: Dr. Mark Palmeri, MEDRhythms

Problem

MEDRhythms incorporates Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation (RAS), a novel approach utilizing rhythmic engagement with the motor system, in improving the rate of recovery of patients undergoing treatment for neurologic injuries and diseases. Our task is to improve their treatment device through the addition of a biometric tracking of fatigue over the course of the therapy.

Our Solution

We will design a way to detect & alert for user fatigue during RAS therapy sessions in a general population of "tech-challenged walkers" that will decrease the occurrence of "high-risk" fatigue-related symptoms and/or fatigue-related injury. A prototype is in the works right now! Come back in a few weeks to see updates on our progress.

GWHT Center

Advisors: Riley Deutsch, Dr. Nimmi Rammanujam

Problem

Founded by Dr. Nimmi Rammanujam, the Center for Global Women's Health Technologies focuses on advancing research and global health to create more accessible and effective cancer diagnosis, prevention, and treatment for women worldwide. A group at the lab is incorporating novel fluoroscopy techniques into the CapCell, a low-cost and portable device used to detect breast tumor residuals that could lead to cancer recurrence. Before the device can be taken into the clinic, the optimal lighting conditions must be researched, which requires a convenient and reliable control of the device's electronic components.

Our Solution

I have developed a circuit for the CapCell that allows for the LEDs involved in the fluoroscopy imaging process to be driven at a constant current. It is accompanied by a easy analog and digital brightness control by a graphical user interface (GUI) and is currently being tested for efficacy in the lab.

Ultrasound Denoising

Collaborator: Corey Simmerer

Problem

Ultrasounds are commonly used in medical imaging, especially fetal imaging, due to its low cost, convenience, and non-ionizing properties. From the inherent properties of ultrasound waves, however, backscattering of the tissue undergoes random constructive and destructive interference that create an unwanted textured or grainy visual effect and diminish the quality of the image. This noise is categorized as speckle noise.

Our Solution

We explored novel algorithms that are commonly applied to reduce speckle noise and improve image quality of ultrasound images. The median filter and discrete wavelet transform denoising methods were implemented on noisy fetal and carotid artery images, and the denoised outputs were compared to the original image. You can read more about our experimental process and view our results here.

Spirometer

Collaborator: Jason Stranne

Introduction to Spirometry

Spirometers are commonly used to assess lung function and diagnose respiratory conditions, such as asthma or COPD. It primarily measures air flow as a proxy for lung function.

Our Design

We built a spirometer that can measure and report air flow and lung volume from a single inhalation and exhalation. Our device collects pressure data from a pneumotachometer sensor, pre-processes analog data, reports the air messurements of interest on an LCD shield, and graphically displays the flow-volume curve to visually aid lung function assessment. After prototyping, our spirometer was tested against a clinical spirometer, and the measurements were found to be within the limits of agreement in a Bland-Altman analysis.
